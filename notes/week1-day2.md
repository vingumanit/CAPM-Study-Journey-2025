# ðŸ“Œ 1. The 12 PMBOK 7 Project Management Principles

### **1. Stewardship**
Be a responsible **steward** of resources, trust, and the environment.

### **2. Team**
Foster **collaboration**, **respect**, and a **high-performance** culture.

### **3. Stakeholders**
Engage **stakeholders** proactively throughout the project.

### **4. Value**
Focus on delivering **value** and measurable outcomes, not just outputs.

### **5. Systems Thinking**
See the project as part of a **larger system** with interdependencies.

### **6. Leadership**
Lead with **vision**, **empathy**, and **adaptability**.

### **7. Tailoring**
**Tailor** methods, practices, and tools to the project context.

### **8. Quality**
Build **quality** into processes and deliverables from the start.

### **9. Complexity**
Recognize and actively manage **complexity** in people, systems, and project environments.

### **10. Risk**
Identify, analyze, and respond to **risk** proactively.

### **11. Adaptability and Resilience**
Be **adaptive** and **resilient** in the face of uncertainty and change.

### **12. Change**
Support **organizational change** needed to realize project benefits.

# ðŸ“Œ 2. The 5 Process Groups

### **1. Initiating**
Define the project, identify major **stakeholders**, and create the **Project Charter**.

### **2. Planning**
Develop the **Project Management Plan**, **baselines**, and subsidiary plans.

### **3. Executing**
Perform the work defined in the plan; manage teams, communications, and quality.

### **4. Monitoring & Controlling**
Track performance against **baselines**, manage **changes**, and control deviations.

### **5. Closing**
Finalize deliverables, obtain acceptance, release resources, and document **lessons learned**.

# ðŸ“Œ 3. Key Documents

## **A. Project Charter**
The **Project Charter**:

- Formally **authorizes** the project  
- Gives the **Project Manager authority** to use resources  
- Defines **high-level scope**, **milestones**, **budget**, and **risks**  
- Identifies key **stakeholders**  
- Links the project to the **business case**  

Created during **Initiating** and approved by the **Sponsor**.

---

## **B. Scope Baseline**
The **Scope Baseline** is the approved scope and includes:

- **Project Scope Statement**  
- **WBS (Work Breakdown Structure)**  
- **WBS Dictionary**  

Used during **Monitoring & Controlling** to compare scope performance.

---

## **C. Communications Management Plan**
The **Communications Management Plan**:

- Identifies **what** will be communicated  
- Specifies **who** receives information  
- Defines **frequency**, **format**, and **methods**  
- Establishes **roles** and **responsibilities**  
- Provides **escalation paths**  

Created during **Planning**.
