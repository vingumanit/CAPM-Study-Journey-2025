# ✅ CAPM Domain I Summary: Project Management Fundamentals (PMBOK 7 Ch. 1–2)

---

## ⭐ 1. What is a Project?
A **project** is a temporary endeavor that creates a **unique** product, service, or result.

---

## ⭐ 2. Project Management
**Project Management** is the application of knowledge, skills, tools, and techniques to meet project requirements and deliver **value**.

---

## ⭐ 3. Project Life Cycle

A **project life cycle** is the series of phases a project goes through from start to finish.

### **Typical Life Cycle Phases**
- **Initiation** – Define purpose, high-level scope, and stakeholders.
- **Planning** – Develop scope, schedule, cost, risk, communication, and quality plans.
- **Execution** – Perform the work; manage team, communication, and quality.
- **Monitoring & Controlling** – Measure performance, track risks, approve changes.
- **Closure** – Final deliverables, resource release, and lessons learned.

### **Two Main Life Cycle Types**
- **Predictive (Waterfall)** – Requirements fixed, scope well-defined.
- **Adaptive (Agile/Iterative)** – Requirements evolve; frequent feedback.

---

## ⭐ 4. Project Management Processes

Even though PMBOK 7 is principle-based, CAPM still tests understanding of **Process Groups**.

### **The 5 Process Groups**
1. **Initiating**
2. **Planning**
3. **Executing**
4. **Monitoring & Controlling**
5. **Closing**

### **Important Process Concepts**
- **Deliverables** – Measurable outputs.
- **Requirements** – Stakeholder needs.
- **Constraints** – Scope–Schedule–Cost (Iron Triangle).
- **Assumptions** – Things believed true for planning.
- **Change Requests** – Formal modifications to the plan.
- **Baselines** – Approved versions of scope, schedule, and cost.

---

## ⭐ 5. PMBOK 7: The 12 Project Management Principles

1. **Stewardship** – Responsible, ethical use of resources.  
2. **Team** – Build a collaborative, high-performing environment.  
3. **Stakeholders** – Engage stakeholders proactively.  
4. **Value** – Deliver outcomes that provide value.  
5. **Systems Thinking** – Understand the broader environment.  
6. **Leadership** – Lead with vision, adaptability, and empathy.  
7. **Tailoring** – Adapt approaches based on context.  
8. **Quality** – Build quality into processes and outputs.  
9. **Complexity** – Address and navigate complexity.  
10. **Risk** – Manage uncertainty and threats/opportunities.  
11. **Adaptability & Resilience** – Respond effectively to change.  
12. **Change** – Enable and support organizational change.

---

## ⭐ 6. Project Roles (Key CAPM Concepts)

### **Project Manager (PM)**
- Accountable for meeting objectives.
- Integrates work across teams.
- Manages scope, schedule, cost, risks, quality, and stakeholders.

### **Project Team Members**
- Execute tasks.
- Provide estimates.
- Identify risks and issues.

### **Sponsor**
- Provides funding and strategic direction.
- Approves major decisions.

### **Stakeholders**
- Anyone affected by the project.
- Require active communication and engagement.

### **Product Owner (Agile)**
- Owns backlog and prioritizes value.

### **Scrum Master (Agile)**
- Removes blockers and facilitates team performance.

## ⭐ 7. Five Real-World Examples (Exam-Friendly)

### **Example 1 – Building an E-Commerce Website**
- Initiation → Identify stakeholders.
- Planning → Define scope.
- Execution → Development and UI design.
- Monitoring & Controlling → Track progress; handle change requests.
- Closure → Launch website.

### **Example 2 – Implementing a New HR System**
- Predictive approach with stable requirements.
- High stakeholder engagement.
- Manage risks (e.g., data migration).

### **Example 3 – Marketing Campaign (Adaptive)**
- Weekly iterations.
- Product Owner prioritizes tasks.
- Scrum Master removes blockers.

### **Example 4 – Construction of a Warehouse (Predictive)**
- Strict baselines and change control.
- Collaboration with engineers and regulators.

### **Example 5 – Developing a Mobile App**
- Hybrid approach (Agile UI + Predictive backend).
- Frequent stakeholder demos improve value.
